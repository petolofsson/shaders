## Candidates evaluated

### 1. CHROMA_STR from HWY_ACHROM_FRAC (x=202)

**Verdict: NEEDS-MORE-DATA**

#### Signal availability
`achrom_frac` is computed in `analysis_frame.fx:340` as the fraction of the 32×18
downsample grid pixels with Oklab C < 0.05. It is EMA-smoothed into `MeanChromaTex.a`
(`analysis_frame.fx:349`, alpha ≈ 0.083 at 60fps → ~12-frame time constant), then
written to HWY slot x=202 by `DebugOverlayPS` at `analysis_frame.fx:197-198`. The
signal is live and readable via `ReadHWY(HWY_ACHROM_FRAC)` — no new passes needed.

#### Current chroma_str_base formula
`grade.fx:366-368` (BuildSceneCtx):
```hlsl
ctx.chroma_str_base = CHROMA_STR * 0.04
                    * lerp(0.80, 1.20, smoothstep(0.05, 0.35, ctx.zone_log_key))
                    * lerp(1.2, 1.0, saturate(mean_C_scene / 0.12));
```
Two adaptive layers are already applied before this candidate would add a third:
- **Zone key modulation** (zone_log_key): dark/muted scenes → ×1.20 boost; bright scenes → ×0.80.
- **R151 mean_C modulation** (HWY_MEAN_CHROMA x=198): low scene chroma → ×1.2 boost; high chroma → ×1.0 clamp.

#### Direction conflict with R151
Both R151 and the proposed achrom_frac modulation fire in the same gray-heavy-scene scenario,
but in **opposite directions**:

| Condition | R151 (existing) | Proposed achrom_frac |
|-----------|----------------|----------------------|
| Gray-heavy / overcast scene | mean_C_scene low → ×1.2 (boost) | achrom_frac high → ×0.70 (suppress) |
| Vivid scene | mean_C_scene high → ×1.0 | achrom_frac low → ×1.0 |

In a gray-heavy scene the net product would be approximately 1.2 × 0.70 = 0.84 — a mild
suppression relative to the knob value. The two terms partially cancel, which raises the
question of which direction is actually correct for the use case. R151 was introduced
to prevent chroma lift from flattening already-muted scenes into further muddiness
(lifting muted → lift more). The proposed achrom_frac rule claims the opposite (gray-heavy →
lift less). These cannot both be right at the same time without a more nuanced target.

**The contradiction must be resolved before implementation.** Options:
- Remove or invert the R151 lerp direction and replace with achrom_frac (they are not
  identical signals but are highly correlated in practice).
- Keep both and narrow R151's range so they compose without excessive cancellation.
- Define a single combined gray-scene signal that drives a single multiplier.

#### Framing sensitivity
`achrom_frac` is a global pixel-fraction statistic. Unlike `zone_log_key` (a 4×4
spatial mean of zone medians, which integrates over the full frame uniformly), achrom_frac
responds immediately to compositional changes within a scene — a pan from a gray
concrete wall to a colorful storefront changes achrom_frac substantially without a
scene cut. The EMA smoothing (α ≈ 0.083 at 60fps) provides a ~12-frame lag, but
unlike zone stats (which get Kalman scene-cut override via `HWY_SCENE_CUT`), achrom_frac
has no scene-cut reset path in the highway write (`analysis_frame.fx:197-198` reads
`MeanChromaTex.a` from the previous frame without scene-cut gating).

This is the "fluctuates with scene content, not just neutrality" risk the job spec flags.
It could manifest as slow chroma-strength pumping during camera movement across mixed
interiors/exteriors.

#### Feasibility summary
The mechanism is self-limiting (smooth lerp, no hard gate or threshold seam). The
formula `lerp(1.0, 0.70, achrom_frac)` at grade.fx:366 after the existing multipliers
is architecturally straightforward. Cost is one `ReadHWY(HWY_ACHROM_FRAC)` call in
`BuildSceneCtx()`, already on-chip.

**Blocked by:** (a) direction conflict with R151 needs resolution, (b) need at least
one gray-scene / overcast playthrough to confirm that gray-heavy frames are subjectively
over-saturated after R151 is in effect — the hypothesis is not yet validated against
observed artifact.

#### Implementation sketch (if approved after data collection)
In `BuildSceneCtx()`, after `grade.fx:368`:
```hlsl
float achrom_frac = ReadHWY(HWY_ACHROM_FRAC);
ctx.chroma_str_base *= lerp(1.0, 0.70, achrom_frac);
```
The HWY_CHROMA_STR highway writer (`grade.fx:655-659`) would also need updating to
include the achrom_frac term for consistency (currently it omits even the R151
mean_C modulation — pre-existing discrepancy).
