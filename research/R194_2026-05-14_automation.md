### 1. CHROMA_STR from HWY_ACHROM_FRAC (x=202)

**Verdict: REJECT**

**Hypothesis (from job spec):** `chroma_str *= lerp(1.0, 0.70, achrom_frac)`

**Signal audit:**
- `HWY_ACHROM_FRAC` (slot 202) is correctly written by `analysis_frame.fx:191–192`: reads `.a` channel of `MeanChromaSamp`, which accumulates at `analysis_frame.fx:333` as `achrom_count / float(DS_W * DS_H)` — fraction of pixels with Oklab C < 0.05.
- Temporal smoothing: EMA with α = `lerp(frametime*0.001, 1.0, scene_cut)` — approximately τ ≈ 1s at 60fps, instant on scene cut (`analysis_frame.fx:337,342`). Stable signal.
- `HWY_ACHROM_FRAC` is **not currently read in `grade.fx`** — grep confirms zero references. Consistent with prior reverts.

**Current chroma_str path in `grade.fx`:**
- `grade.fx:405` — `ctx.chroma_str_base = VIBRANCE * 0.04;` — flat scalar from user knob, no scene adaptation
- `grade.fx:606–607` — `chroma_str = ctx.chroma_str_base; chroma_str *= lerp(1.0, 0.65, smoothstep(0.02, 0.08, local_var));` — R68A per-pixel textured-region attenuation applied at use site

**Prior implementation evidence:**

R161 (2026-05-10) implemented an achrom_frac gate on `chroma_str_base`:
```hlsl
* lerp(1.0, 0.60, smoothstep(0.60, 0.85, achrom_frac))
```
40% max attenuation, gated at 0.60–0.85. **Dropped** — documented in R176:
> "R161 was dropped because achrom_frac multiplier on chroma_str_base flattened simultaneous contrast."

**Why the job spec formula has the same problem:**

`lerp(1.0, 0.70, achrom_frac)` differs from R161 only in cap (30% vs 40%) and ramp shape (linear vs smoothstep-gated). The root defect is structural: `achrom_frac` counts near-neutral pixels regardless of scene geometry. A scene with grey concrete architecture and a saturated foreground subject will have high `achrom_frac`, causing the colorful subject to receive 20–30% less chroma lift — the opposite of the intended correction. A smaller cap (30% vs 40%) reduces the damage magnitude but doesn't eliminate the false-positive case.

The 0.05 Oklab C threshold is accurate for genuine neutrals; the problem is that those neutrals may be background, not subject. Scene-level attenuation based on pixel count is too coarse for this purpose.

**Recommendation:** Close this candidate. Update job spec.
